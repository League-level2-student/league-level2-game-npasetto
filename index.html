Eternal RPG

by: Nicholas Pasetto

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I wanted to make a game like Infinity RPG on Roblox. Infinity is a really big game where you constantly get more powerful and find better equipment as you level up. There are lots of different worlds and enemies to fight as well as secret things to find along the way.

2. What I Learned

User testing showed me that there were a lot of bugs in the game. I fixed most of them, but some were harder to fix. Some like the frozen buttons seemed like a problem with Java. Players had trouble in the Void Labyrinth, which was very useful information. So, I made the Void Labyrinth easier.

3. Architecture of My Game

Eternal RPG is a very large game with lots of different parts. You play a hero that fights enemies and moves from world to world, growing more powerful as you go. There are more than one hundred worlds, with many boss fights along the way. Different worlds are locked away behind portals, which usually require a key. Each world has harder enemies and some have additional challenges like mazes and traps. The player can level up and gain power, but there are also many different inventory items - swords, guns, armor, keys, and potions. These items can be found by defeating bosses and buying things in the shop. The player can also "prestige" which resets your progress but gives you experience and gold multipliers.

4. Technical Implementation

I am proud of developing code for a final boss with multiple stages. All the other bosses are just enemies with large amounts of health and damage who do only one thing for the whole fight. For the final boss, I wanted to make him disappear when he gets down to a certain health level. At this point, he summons smaller enemies who attack the player. Then when those enemies are defeated, the boss returns and you fight him more.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was starting it. Making a giant game with many worlds, enemies, and inventory items seemed like a lot. It was helpful to look at League Invaders at the start. It gave me a lot of ideas on how to set up the first parts of the game that I wanted to make.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I were to continue working on this game, I would like the player to have a respawn timer so you don't just respawn instantly. Also, I would want to do more art improvements for the whole game. The menu, the worlds, the monsters, the player, everything. Everything in the code is set up to make it all custom and play well, but some cool art would be awesome.
